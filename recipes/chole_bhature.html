Chole Bhature

Description

Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish that combines spicy, flavorful chickpeas (chole) with crispy, fluffy fried bread (bhature). It’s a beloved street food and is often enjoyed for breakfast or lunch with onions, pickles, and yogurt. The recipe is quite simple to follow but full of rich flavors.

Ingredients

For the Chole (Chickpea Curry):

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (or 2 cups canned chickpeas)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed (or finely chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 4-5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-3 black cardamoms
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1 tsp amchur powder (dry mango powder)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1 tbsp ghee or oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1-2 tbsp tea leaves (optional, for color)

For the Bhature (Fried Bread):

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp semolina (rava or sooji)
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp yogurt
  • 1 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Warm water (as needed to knead the dough)
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps

  1. Step 1:Prepare the Chole (Chickpea Curry)

    1. Soak the chickpeas:
      1. If using dried chickpeas, soak them in plenty of water for 8-10 hours or overnight. After soaking, drain the water.
      2. If using canned chickpeas, simply drain and rinse them under cold water.
    2. Cook the Chickpeas:
      1. In a pressure cooker, add the soaked chickpeas with enough water (about 3 cups). Optionally, add 1-2 tea bags tied in a muslin cloth or a small pouch to give the chole a dark color (this step is optional).
      2. Pressure cook for about 4-5 whistles or until the chickpeas are soft and tender. Set aside.
    3. Prepare the Masala:
      1. Heat 1 tablespoon of ghee or oil in a large pan over medium heat.
      2. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, and black cardamoms. Let them splutter for a few seconds.
      3. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
      4. Add ginger, garlic, and green chilies. Sauté for another minute.
      5. Now, add the pureed tomatoes (or chopped tomatoes) and cook until the oil begins to separate from the masala (around 7-8 minutes).
    4. Spice it Up:
      1. Add the coriander powder, cumin powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, amchur powder, and kasuri methi. Cook for a minute until the spices are well-blended and aromatic.
    5. Add the Cooked Chickpeas:
      1. Add the cooked chickpeas (without the tea bags, if used) to the masala. Stir well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
      2. Add 1-1.5 cups of water (adjust according to the consistency you prefer for the curry). Bring to a boil and then let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
      3. Add garam masala and salt. Adjust the seasoning as needed.
    6. Simmer and Garnish:
      1. Let the chole cook for another 5 minutes to blend the flavors. If you want a thicker gravy, mash a few chickpeas with the back of a spoon.
      2. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.

  2. Step 2: Prepare the Bhature (Fried Bread)

    1. Prepare the Dough:
      1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, semolina, baking soda, sugar, and salt.
      2. Add yogurt, oil (or ghee), and start mixing. Gradually add warm water and knead the dough into a smooth, soft, and elastic consistency. The dough should not be too sticky or too firm.
      3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 1 hour. The dough should rise slightly.
    2. Shape the Bhature:
      1. After the dough has rested, divide it into small balls (about 8-10 balls, depending on the size you want for your bhature).
      2. Roll each ball into a smooth, slightly thick oval or round shape using a rolling pin. Keep the surface lightly floured to prevent sticking.
    3. Fry the Bhature:
      1. Heat oil in a deep frying pan or wok over medium-high heat. To check if the oil is hot enough, drop a small piece of dough into the oil. If it rises immediately, the oil is ready.
      2. Fry each bhatura by gently sliding it into the hot oil. Press the bhatura lightly with a slotted spoon to help it puff up. Flip it once it’s golden brown and cook until both sides are crisp and golden.
      3. Remove from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.

  3. Step 3: Serve

    1. Serve the hot Chole alongside freshly made Bhature.
    2. Garnish with chopped onions, coriander leaves, and a squeeze of lemon.
    3. Enjoy with pickle, yogurt, or raita on the side.
