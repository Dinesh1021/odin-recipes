Paneer Tikka

Description

Paneer Tikka is a popular vegetarian appetizer from Indian cuisine. It is made by marinating chunks of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) in a spiced yogurt mixture, then grilling or baking them until they are crispy and golden on the outside, while remaining soft and tender on the inside.

Ingredients

For the Marinade:

  • 250g Paneer (cubed into 1.5-inch pieces)
  • 3 tbsp thick yogurt (curd)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tbsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 2 tbsp chickpea flour (besan) (optional, for binding)
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil or melted butter (for richness)
  • Salt to taste

For Grilling/serving:

  • 1-2 tbsp oil (for basting)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • 1 onion (sliced into rings, for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)
  • Fresh mint chutney (optional, for serving)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Paneer and Marinade

    1. Prepare the Paneer: Cut the paneer into 1.5-inch cubes. If using store-bought paneer, make sure it's firm and well-drained.
    2. Mix the Marinade: In a mixing bowl, combine the thick yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, cumin powder, kasuri methi, chickpea flour, vegetable oil (or melted butter), and salt. Whisk everything together until smooth.
    3. Marinate the Paneer: Add the paneer cubes into the marinade and gently coat them. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 1-2 hours (overnight is even better for deeper flavor).

  2. Grill the Paneer

    1. Preheat the Grill/Oven: If you’re using a grill or oven, preheat it to 200°C (400°F). Alternatively, you can also make paneer tikka on a stovetop using a griddle (tawa).
    2. Thread the Paneer (optional): If using a skewer, thread the marinated paneer cubes onto the skewers. You can alternate with sliced onions and bell peppers for extra flavor.
    3. Grilling (Oven or Grill): Place the skewers or paneer cubes directly on the grill or baking tray lined with parchment paper. Brush the paneer with oil or butter. Grill for about 15-20 minutes, turning the skewers halfway through, until the paneer is golden brown with slight charring. You can baste it with more oil or butter during grilling for a rich flavor.
    4. Stovetop Griddle: Heat a non-stick or cast iron griddle (tawa) on medium heat. Add a little oil and place the marinated paneer pieces. Cook each side for 2-3 minutes until golden brown and slightly crisp.

  3. Serve

    1. Once grilled, remove the paneer from the skewers (if using) and place them on a serving platter.
    2. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves, onion rings, and lemon wedges.
    3. Serve hot with a side of mint chutney or tamarind chutney.
