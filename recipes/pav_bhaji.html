Pav Bhaji

Description

Pav Bhaji is a beloved street food from Mumbai, India, consisting of a spiced vegetable mash (bhaji) served with buttered, toasted pav (bread rolls). It’s a delicious and satisfying meal, full of flavors and textures. Here’s a simple recipe to make this mouthwatering dish at home.

Ingredients

For the Bhaji (Vegetable Curry):

  • 2 tbsp butter (for cooking + extra for serving)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 large tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, peeled and chopped
  • 1/2 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 large potato, peeled and chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped (adjust to spice preference)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp pav bhaji masala (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1-2 tbsp water (for consistency)

For the Pav (Bread Rolls):

  • 8 pav (bread rolls)
  • 2-3 tbsp butter

Steps

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Bhaji (Vegetable Curry)

    1. Boil the Vegetables:
      1. In a large pot, add the chopped potatoes, carrots, peas, and a pinch of salt. Add water and cook until the vegetables are soft and tender (about 10-15 minutes). You can use a pressure cooker to speed this up, cooking for 2-3 whistles.
      2. Once cooked, drain the water (but keep a little aside) and mash the vegetables using a potato masher. Set aside.
    2. Cook the Base:
      1. Heat 2 tablespoons of butter and 1 tablespoon of oil in a large pan or skillet over medium heat.
      2. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter for a few seconds.
      3. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
      4. Add the ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chilies. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
    3. Cook the Vegetables:
      1. Add the chopped tomatoes and green bell pepper to the pan. Sauté until the tomatoes soften and the oil starts to separate from the masala (about 5-7 minutes).
      2. Add the turmeric powder, red chili powder, pav bhaji masala, and garam masala powder. Stir everything together and cook for a minute until the spices are well-mixed and aromatic.
    4. Mash and Combine:
      1. Add the boiled and mashed vegetables to the pan. Mix well with the masala base.
      2. Add 1-2 tablespoons of water to get the desired consistency. The bhaji should be soft but not too runny.
      3. Adjust the salt and spice levels according to taste. Let the bhaji simmer on low heat for 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend together.
      4. Finish with a squeeze of lemon juice and garnish with chopped coriander leaves.

  2. Step 2: Prepare the Pav (Bread Rolls)

    1. Toast the Pav:
      1. Heat a tawa (griddle) or non-stick pan over medium heat.
      2. Slice the pav rolls horizontally (keeping them intact from one side).
      3. Add a little butter on the pan and place the pav slices on it. Toast the pav until golden brown and crispy on both sides. You can add a bit more butter for extra flavor.

  3. Step 3: Serve

    1. Serve the hot bhaji in a bowl, garnished with fresh coriander leaves, and a dollop of butter on top.
    2. Serve with the toasted pav on the side
    3. Optionally, add a wedge of lemon and some finely chopped onions for garnish and a fresh crunch.
